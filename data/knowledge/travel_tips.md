# Travel Knowledge Base

## General Travel Tips

### Packing Essentials
- Always carry a portable charger for long days of exploration
- Pack layers for unpredictable weather, especially in coastal cities
- Bring comfortable walking shoes; most cities are best explored on foot
- Keep copies of important documents separate from originals

### Budget Management
- Book accommodations 2-3 weeks in advance for best rates
- Use local transit passes instead of taxis for significant savings
- Eat where locals eat; avoid restaurants directly facing major attractions
- Free walking tours often provide the best local insights

### Safety Guidelines
- Register with your embassy for extended international trips
- Keep emergency numbers saved offline on your phone
- Use hotel safes for valuables and passport copies
- Stay aware of common local scams and pickpocket zones

## City-Specific Knowledge

### Barcelona, Spain
- Best time to visit: April-June or September-October (mild weather, fewer crowds)
- Must-see: Sagrada Familia, Park Güell, La Rambla, Gothic Quarter
- Local tip: Book Sagrada Familia tickets online weeks in advance
- Food: Try tapas at La Boqueria market, paella should only be eaten at lunch
- Transit: T-Casual card offers 10 trips on metro/bus, more economical than single tickets
- Neighborhoods: Gràcia for local vibe, Barceloneta for beach, El Born for nightlife

### Paris, France
- Best time to visit: April-May or September-October
- Must-see: Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Montmartre, Notre-Dame area
- Local tip: Museum Pass saves money and skip-the-line access for 2+ days
- Food: Croissants are breakfast only; dinner starts after 7:30 PM
- Transit: Carnet of 10 metro tickets is cheaper than buying individually
- Neighborhoods: Le Marais for history, Saint-Germain for cafés, Belleville for authentic Paris

### Tokyo, Japan
- Best time to visit: March-May (cherry blossoms) or October-November (fall colors)
- Must-see: Senso-ji Temple, Shibuya Crossing, Meiji Shrine, Tsukiji Outer Market
- Local tip: Get a Suica/Pasmo card immediately for seamless transit
- Food: Conveyor belt sushi for budget, department store basements for variety
- Transit: JR Pass only worthwhile for multiple city trips; Tokyo Metro pass for city-only
- Etiquette: No tipping, no eating while walking, quiet on trains

### New York City, USA
- Best time to visit: April-June or September-November
- Must-see: Central Park, Statue of Liberty, Times Square, Brooklyn Bridge
- Local tip: TKTS booth for same-day Broadway discounts
- Food: Pizza by the slice, bagels for breakfast, diverse neighborhoods for cuisine
- Transit: Unlimited MetroCard for 7 days if using subway frequently
- Neighborhoods: SoHo for shopping, Williamsburg for hipster vibe, Harlem for jazz

### London, UK
- Best time to visit: May-September (warmest, but still pack layers)
- Must-see: British Museum (free), Tower of London, Westminster, Borough Market
- Local tip: Many world-class museums are free; prioritize paid attractions strategically
- Food: Full English breakfast, afternoon tea experience, curry on Brick Lane
- Transit: Oyster card or contactless payment; avoid Zone 1 during rush hour
- Neighborhoods: Shoreditch for street art, Camden for markets, South Bank for culture

## Weather Considerations

### Tropical Destinations
- Rainy season often means afternoon showers, mornings still enjoyable
- Pack quick-dry clothing and waterproof bags for electronics
- Book outdoor activities for morning slots

### European Winter
- Christmas markets peak mid-December, book accommodations early
- Daylight hours short; plan indoor activities for afternoons
- Layer with thermal base layers for extended outdoor time

### Desert Climates
- Temperature swings can be 20°C between day and night
- Hydration critical; carry water at all times
- Early morning and late afternoon best for outdoor activities

## Itinerary Planning Principles

### Day Structure
- Start with high-energy activities in the morning
- Build in rest time after lunch (especially in warm climates)
- Save flexible activities for late afternoon
- Book dinner reservations for popular restaurants

### Multi-Day Trips
- Don't over-schedule; 2-3 major activities per day maximum
- Group nearby attractions on the same day
- Include one "slow" day for every 3-4 active days
- Keep last day light for packing and unexpected delays

### Family Travel
- Build in playground/park time for children
- Choose accommodations with kitchens for meal flexibility
- Have backup indoor activities for weather changes
- Shorter daily walking distances than adult-only trips
